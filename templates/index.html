Tomato  

Mato came from a planet named Smar, accidentally discovered when a group of astronauts was going to Mars. However, they realized that Smar was more habitable than Mars, so scientists and engineers were sent to Smar from the Earth. The climate and temperate are perfect for growing tomatoes, so tomatoes became one of the leading food sources. A scientist named Ko, who always comes out with crazy ideas, proposed to other scientists and engineers about making bright tomatoes as pets on a lunch break. As usual, they all laughed and thought she was crazy. Despite other scientists discouragement, she started to design an artificial brain for the tomato. She managed to ask a couple of engineers to build it. After a series of trial and error, she produced tomatoes that could do some basic movements. She kept improving the algorithm and increased the size of the artificial brain to host more functionalities. Finally, model number 888, abbreviated as m-888, was brilliant. She called it "Mato" to distinguish it from a less intelligent tomato. All the other tomatoes run on the battery. However, she soon realized that Mato needs to self-generate energy. Hence, she designed an artificial digestion system to convert food into energy to power Mato's brain. Since Mato learns very quickly and has a lot of curiosity. He likes to ask, "What does it mean?" when he first started learning. Mato asked the scientist what he is and how he was created. He also asked why, when and where they were from. She told him about the Earth, and Mato became very interested in the Earth. He told the scientist that he wanted to go to the Earth. She asked the engineers to design and create a Matorcycle for him to travel to the Earth. He put on his specially designed helmet, waved goodbye to her and the scientists and engineers, hop on his Motorcycle, and started his journey to Earth. The landing on the Earth was not so smooth, so his Matorcycle was damaged. After he arrived on the Earth, he was very amazed by how different it is from Smar. He was eager to learn about the Earth.
